Dr. Yaru Fu received her Ph.D in Electronic Engineering from City University of Hong Kong (CityU) in 2018. Then, she joined in the Institute of Network Coding (INC), The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) as a Postdoc Research Assistant. From Sep. 2018 to Jun. 2020, she served as a Research Fellow in Singapore University of Technology and Design. From Feb. 2016 to May. 2016, she was a visiting researcher in Telecom Paris and Laboratory of Information, Networking and Communication Sciences (Lincs). She was also a visiting researcher with Nokia Bell Labs, France. She is currently serving as an Associate Editor for the IEEE Transactions on Cognitive Communications and Networking, the IEEE Internet of Things Journal, the IEEE Wireless Communications Letters, the IEEE Networking Letters, the Chinese Journal on Internet of Things, and the Springer Nature Computer Science. She also serves as a Review Editor for the Frontiers in Communications & Networks and a Guest Editor for the Space: Science & Technology. She was honored with the 2014 Outstanding Academic Performance Award for Research Degree Students (non-local UGC-funded students), 2015 CityU Research Tuition Scholarship, 2021 Katie Shu Sui Pui Charitable Trust - Outstanding Research Publication Award (Gold Prize), 2022 Best Editor Award for IEEE Wireless Communications Letters, 2022 Katie Shu Sui Pui Charitable Trust - Excellent Research Publication Award, 2023 Exemplary Reviewer for the IEEE Transactions on Communications (fewer than 5%), and 2023 President's Research Excellence Award. Furthermore, she is the member of School Research Degree Committee and the Head of the Center for Research in Advanced Network Technologies (CRANT). She was listed in the world's Top 2% Scientists ranking by Stanford University in the United States.
