Dr. Yaru Fu received her Ph.D in Electronic Engineering from City University of Hong Kong (CityU) in 2018. Then, she joined in the Institute of Network Coding (INC), The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) as a Postdoc Research Assistant. From Sep. 2018 to Jun. 2020, she served as a Research Fellow in Singapore University of Technology and Design. From Feb. 2016 to May. 2016, she was a visiting researcher in Telecom ParisTech (TPT) and Laboratory of Information, Networking and Communication Sciences (Lincs). She was also a visiting researcher with Nokia Bell Labs, Paris, France.

She is currently serving as an Associate Editor for the IEEE Wireless Communications Letters, an Associate Editor for the IEEE Networking Letters, and a Review Editor for the Frontiers in Communications and Networks. She was honored with the 2021 Katie Shu Sui Pui Charitable Trust - Outstanding Research Publication Award (Gold Prize) and the 2022 Best Editor Award for IEEE Wireless Communications Letters. Furthermore, she is the member of School Research Degree Committee and the Head of the Center for Advanced Network Technologies (CRANT).
